## Conclusion

The division-count data support a clear crossover in the post-jamming regime. By $\phi_2$, the cumulative number of divisions between $\phi_J$ and $\phi_2$ is about $0.05-0.08\,N_J$ for no feedback and $P_0=10^{-1}$, remains appreciable at $P_0=10^{-2}$, drops to about $10^{-2}\,N_J$ at $P_0=10^{-3}$, and is nearly zero for $P_0=10^{-4}$ to $10^{-5}$.

This supports two mechanical regimes. For weak feedback, ongoing proliferation still creates a non-negligible number of new cells and therefore continues to inject new degrees of freedom. In that regime, time-dependent, growth-driven rheological effects of the kind discussed for growing active matter can remain important.

For strong feedback, growth is not absent, but division-mediated creation of new cells is strongly suppressed. In that limit, the mechanics are better viewed as mostly quasistatic: rigidity increases mainly through biomass accumulation, completion of the pre-existing free-bud reservoir, and loading or creation of contacts in the existing force-bearing network, rather than through repeated mother-daughter separation.

Put differently, weak feedback is the regime where growth-driven rheology is most visible, whereas in the low-$P_0$ limit these effects decay because persistent division no longer dominates the post-jamming dynamics.
