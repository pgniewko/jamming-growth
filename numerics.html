Numerics Reference

This page records only the quantities and constructions used in the paper: φ2, P2, G2, the mean compressed-sector flux fraction ̄λc, and the growth-rate crossover observables.

1. Measured Secondary Arrest

The paper defines the secondary-arrest density by

u(φ2, P0) = ε

The current figure construction uses active cutoff 1, so the measured arrest row is the first saved post-jam row with

n_initial_free_active <= 1

At that row the code reads

phi2 = row.phi P2 = row.pressure

2. Predicted Arrest Density

The predicted secondary-arrest density used in the paper is

phi2_pred = phi_J + n_J * (chi_bar * a_eps + (1 - chi_bar) * u_J * integral_s)

Here phi_J, n_J, and u_J are taken at jamming, chi_bar is the arithmetic mean of chi_c over saved post-jam rows with φ <= φ2, and a_eps and integral_s come from the Kaplan-Meier construction for the completion-threshold survival function.

3. Kaplan-Meier Construction

The paper models depletion through the survival function

S(a) = Prob(an initially free bud is still active beyond delta-area a)

The Kaplan-Meier construction uses initially free buds, treating mechanical completion as the event and division or run termination as censoring. From that stepwise survival curve it extracts:

  • a_eps: the first delta-area where survival falls to the target level active_cutoff / initial_free_total
  • integral_s: the area under S(a) up to that crossing

4. Mean Compressed-Sector Flux Fraction

The paper defines the compressed-sector flux fraction by

u_total = Nu / (N - Nf) lambda_c = ((1 - u_total) * chi_c) / (u_total + (1 - u_total) * chi_c)

The inset in Fig. 3B uses the arithmetic mean over saved post-jam rows up to arrest:

lambda_c_bar = (1 / M) * sum_k lambda_c(phi_k), over all saved rows with phi_k <= phi2

5. Arrest-Point Shear Modulus

The paper figure uses the saved PHI2 shear outputs. For each (P0, seed), it keeps one representative arrest packing and estimates the shear modulus from the initial topology-preserving segment of the stress-strain data:

G2 = slope of stress vs strain over the initial topology-preserving segment

The summary panels use one representative value per seed and then aggregate across seeds.

6. Growth-Rate Crossover Observables

At fixed Δφ = 3 × 10-3, the paper uses two observables:

  • the normalized growth-rate heterogeneity h_gamma = Var(g) / mean(g)^2
  • the pooled histogram Π(g) of normalized bud growth rates g

These are the quantities shown in the supplementary growth-crossover figure.
