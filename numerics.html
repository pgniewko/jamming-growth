Numerics Reference

This page records the quantities and constructions used here: φ2, P2, G2, the mean compressed-sector flux fraction ̄λc, the growth-rate crossover observables, and the post-jamming division-count diagnostics.

1. Measured Secondary Arrest

The secondary-arrest density is defined by

u(φ2, P0) = ε

The construction uses active cutoff 1, so the measured arrest row is the first saved post-jam row with

n_initial_free_active <= 1

At that row the code reads

phi2 = row.phi P2 = row.pressure

2. Predicted Arrest Density

The predicted secondary-arrest density is

rho_J = (N_J - N_f,J) / L^2 phi2_pred = phi_J + rho_J * (chi_bar * a_eps + (1 - chi_bar) * u_J * integral_s)

Here phi_J, rho_J, and u_J are taken at jamming. rho_J is the non-floating cell density used in the calculation. chi_bar is the arithmetic mean of chi_c over saved post-jam rows with φ <= φ2, and a_eps and integral_s come from the Kaplan-Meier construction for the completion-threshold survival function.

3. Kaplan-Meier Construction

The paper models depletion through the survival function

S(a) = Prob(an initially free bud is still active beyond delta-area a)

The Kaplan-Meier construction uses initially free buds, treating mechanical completion as the event and division or run termination as censoring. From that stepwise survival curve it extracts:

  • a_eps: the first delta-area where survival falls to the target level active_cutoff / initial_free_total
  • integral_s: the area under S(a) up to that crossing

4. Mean Compressed-Sector Flux Fraction

The compressed-sector flux fraction is defined by

u_total = Nu / (N - Nf) lambda_c = ((1 - u_total) * chi_c) / (u_total + (1 - u_total) * chi_c)

The reported value is the arithmetic mean over saved post-jam rows up to arrest:

lambda_c_bar = (1 / M) * sum_k lambda_c(phi_k), over all saved rows with phi_k <= phi2

5. Arrest-Point Shear Modulus

The calculation uses the saved PHI2 shear outputs. For each (P0, seed), it keeps one representative arrest packing and estimates the shear modulus from the initial topology-preserving segment of the stress-strain data:

G2 = slope(delta_shear_stress vs strain) over the initial topology-preserving segment, capped at the first 20 rows

The summaries use one representative value per seed and report the median with bootstrap median error after filtering invalid or outlying values.

6. Growth-Rate Crossover Observables

At fixed Δφ = 3 × 10-3, the calculation uses two observables:

  • the normalized growth-rate heterogeneity h_gamma = Var(g) / mean(g)^2
  • the pooled histogram Π(g) of normalized bud growth rates g

The normalized bud growth rate is g = effective_growth_rate / innate_growth_rate.

7. Post-Jamming Division Counts

The lineage diagnostic quantifies when post-jamming division activity accumulates, relative to the saved post-jam origin φJ and the observed depletion point φ2.

N_div_all(Delta phi) = # { DIVLOG events with phi_event - phi_J <= Delta phi } r_div_all(Delta phi) = Delta N_div_all / Delta phi N_div_track(Delta phi) = # { TRANSITIONS events with event_code = 3 and phi_event - phi_J <= Delta phi }

The reference point phi_J is the first saved row of the matching POSTJAMM_SUMMARY file. Division and tracked-reinjection events are counted from the saved post-jam start, using the event filter event_step >= step_J and event_phi >= phi_J.

The observed depletion point for this diagnostic is the first saved post-jam row with n_initial_free_active == 0. For runs that reach that row, the summary value shown in the lower panel is the cumulative post-jamming division count evaluated at that same point:

N_div(phi2) = N_div_all(Delta phi_2_obs)

This is therefore a direct slice through the cumulative curve at the run's own observed phi_2. Runs that do not exhaust by the saved endpoint remain in the cumulative curves, but they are excluded from the phi_2-slice summary because phi_2 is not observed for them.

The diagnostic uses two summary views:

Panel A: raw cumulative count N_div_all(Delta phi) for L = 20 Panel B: normalized count N_div(phi2) / <N_J> for L = 20

The all-division cumulative curves are summarized across runs by median and interquartile range. The lower panel shows the cumulative cell divisions accumulated between phi_J and phi_2, reported as the median with a bootstrap confidence interval over runs. The tracked subset remains part of the diagnostic tables as a direct check on the reinjection pathway.

8. Vibrational Density of States

The spectral check diagonalizes the vibrational spectrum of each mechanically arrested packing. Rattlers are discarded; every remaining cell carries the same three rigid-body coordinates used to generate the packings, q = (x, y, θ), where (x, y) is the mother–bud centroid and θ the cell orientation (the bud is rigidly collinear, so it adds no independent coordinate).

The elastic energy is a sum of harmonic repulsions over inter-cell lobe–lobe contacts,

U = sum_contacts (k / 2) * (R_a + R_b - r)^2, for r < R_a + R_b

with unit stiffness, lobe radii R_a, and lobe-center separation r under periodic minimum-image. The dynamical matrix is the mass-weighted Hessian evaluated at the force-balanced state, including both the spring-stiffness and the prestress (initial-stress) terms, since the latter is not negligible near marginality:

H v = omega^2 M v

H is the full Hessian, built by finite differences of the analytic energy gradient on the contact network frozen at the reference configuration. M is the consistent dumbbell mass matrix at fixed areal density: the mother lobe has unit mass and the bud lobe mass m_bud = (R_bud / R_mother)^2. Because the reference point is the area-weighted centroid—which coincides with the center of mass—the translation and rotation contributions decouple: within each cell's (x, y, θ) block the x–θ and y–θ off-diagonals are exactly zero, so M is diagonal. Its rotational entry is the rigid-body moment of inertia

I = sum_a m_a * ( l_a^2 + (1/2) * R_a^2 )

i.e. the parallel-axis term plus each disk's own self-spin, with l_a the offset of lobe a from the centroid. Treating the lobes as point masses—dropping the (1/2) R_a^2 self-spin—would underestimate this inertia, by up to roughly a factor of 10 for small buds (α ≈ 1.2), and shift the rotational modes; the self-spin term removes that error.

The two uniform-translation zero modes are removed; the remaining eigenvalues give the mode frequencies omega_n = sqrt(lambda_n) and the density of states D(omega). From the spectrum the diagnostic reads:

  • the count of quasi-zero modes N_zero (lambda < lambda_tol), which tracks the independently measured unconstrained-bud number N_u;
  • the band edge omega*, the onset of the extended band, which scales with the modified excess coordination Delta Z = Z - (6 - 2u) rather than the naive count Z - 6.
